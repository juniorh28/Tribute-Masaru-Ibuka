Masaru Ibuka

Founder of Sony Corporation

Time line of Masaru Ibuka

  • 1908 - Born in Nikko City, Tochigi Prefecture, Japan, April 11.
  • 1933 - Graduated from Waseda University in Science and Engineering.
  • 1933 - Invented a modulated-light transmission system.
  • 1941 - Worked at a photo-chemical laboratory where he researched sound recording technology.
  • 1945 - Founded Tokyo-Tsushin Kenkyusho (Tokyo Telecommunications Laboratory) Co.
  • 1946 - Founded Tokyo-Tsushin-Kogyo Co. (Tokyo Telecommunications Engineering Corporation) with Akio Morita.
  • 1949 - Developed magnetic recording tapes.
  • 1954 - Sony became the first licensee – from the Western Electric Company – of the patent for transistors outside the United States. Ibuka and his team set out to make transistor radios, the first companies to make transistor technology for non-military use.
  • 1958 - Company name officially changed to Sony Corporation.
  • 1971 - Published book, "Kindergarten is too late!", where he argues the best time to develope a child's mind is within the first three years of life.
  • 1976 - Left Sony but stayed on as a close advisor
  • 1997 - Dies of heart failure at the age 89.
